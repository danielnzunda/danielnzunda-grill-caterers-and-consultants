    1. Entrepreneurship

    The process of initiating a business venture, organising the necessary resources and assuming the associated risks and rewards.

    2. Who is an Entrepreneur?

    The one creating a new business in the fare of risk and uncertainity for the purpose of achieving profit and growth by identifying significant and assembling the necessary resources to capitalise on them.

    3. The student should understand the entrepreneurship characteristics such as:

    (a) Desire for responsibility

    (b) Reference for moderate risk

    (c) Confidence on their ability to succeed

    (d) Desire for immediate feedback

    (e) High level of energy

    (f) Future orientation

    (g) Skill at organising

    (h) Value achievement over money

    4. The benefits of entrepreneurship

    (a) Opportunity to create your own destiny

    (b) Opportunity to make a difference

    (c) Opportunity to reach your full potential

    (d) Opportunity to keep impressive profits

    (e) Opportunity to contribute to society and become recognised for your efforts.

    (f) Opportunity to do what you enjoy and have join at it.

    5. Potential drawbacks of entrepreneurship

    (a) Uncertainty of income

    (b) Risks of losing your entire investment

    (c) Long hours and hard work

    (d) Lower quality of life until the business gets established

    (e) High level of stress

    (f) Complete responsibility

    (g) Discouragement



    6. Types of entrepreneurship

    (a) Opportunity entrepreneurs

    (b) Necessity entrepreneurs

    (c) Serial entrepreneurs

    (d) Social entrepreneurs

    (e) Copreneurs
